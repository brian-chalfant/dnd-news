World News Headlines – June 15, 2026

1. US-Iran Peace Deal Reached
A tentative agreement to end the war between the United States and Iran has been reached. An official signing ceremony is scheduled for June 19 in Switzerland. The pact calls for the "immediate and permanent termination of military operations." It aims to reopen the Strait of Hormuz and provide sanctions relief, though nuclear negotiations are deferred to a later date.

2. 2026 FIFA World Cup Continues
The global soccer tournament, which commenced on June 11, is in full swing. Cape Verde made a stunning World Cup debut, securing a draw against Spain. Meanwhile, host cities like Kansas City are gearing up for major group stage matchups, including Argentina vs. Algeria.

3. Fatal Skydiver Plane Crash in Missouri
Officials have confirmed a tragic incident in Butler, Missouri, where a plane crash on Sunday, June 14, resulted in the deaths of all 12 people on board, including the pilot and 11 skydivers.

4. Georgia Gubernatorial and Senate Race Developments
In domestic U.S. politics, gubernatorial candidates in Georgia are making final campaign pitches ahead of run-off elections. President Donald Trump has issued an 11th-hour endorsement for Mike Collins in the Georgia Republican U.S. Senate race.

5. America250 Celebrations Preparations
Preparations are ramping up for the U.S. semiquincentennial (250th anniversary). Key events announced include a "Block Party" concert in Los Angeles on July 4, featuring performances by Chris Stapleton and The Smashing Pumpkins to launch a new charity initiative called "Giving 4th." In Philadelphia, a time capsule scheduled to be opened in the year 2276 has been sealed.
